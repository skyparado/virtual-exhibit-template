Main Exhibits

THE ORIGIN:

The earliest forms of computer data storage and processing. Explore how punch cards encoded data as physical patterns of holes, and how magnetic drums served as the first electronic memory devices before the era of hard drives.

›

THE DISK:

The evolution of magnetic disk storage, from IBM's revolutionary RAMAC 350 in 1956 to today's multi-terabyte hard drives. Discover how spinning platters, magnetic heads, and exponential density improvements defined decades of computing.

›

THE OPTICAL AGE:

The shift from magnetic to optical storage — CDs, DVDs, and Blu-ray. See how laser beams read microscopic pits and lands on a reflective disc surface, and explore an interactive encoder that shows how your text becomes light and shadow on a disc track.

›

THE FLASH:

The revolution of solid-state storage. From NAND flash architecture to the PCIe NVMe protocol, explore how eliminating moving parts allowed storage to become faster, smaller, and more reliable than ever before.

›

THE HORIZON:

Cloud infrastructure, DNA storage, and the technologies being developed today that may define storage for the next century. From distributed datacenters to encoding data in biological molecules, this is where storage is headed.
